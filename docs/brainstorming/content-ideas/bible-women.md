# Major Women of the Bible (Ordered by Book, with Short Summaries)

> Back to [Documentation Hub](../../INDEX.md) | [Content Ideas](./)

This document lists the **major women of the Bible**, arranged in canonical order by book.
Each entry includes a brief summary of the woman's role and significance in the biblical narrative.
Scripture references are based on the **King James Version (KJV)**.

---

## Old Testament

### **Genesis**

- **Eve** (Genesis 2–3)
  The first woman, created by God as Adam's wife, whose actions in the fall introduced sin into the world and highlighted humanity's need for redemption.

- **Sarah** (Genesis 12–23)
  Wife of Abraham and mother of Isaac, through whom God fulfilled His covenant promise of a chosen lineage.

- **Hagar** (Genesis 16; 21)
  Sarah's servant and the mother of Ishmael, whose story demonstrates God's awareness of and care for the afflicted.

- **Rebekah** (Genesis 24–27)
  Wife of Isaac and mother of Jacob and Esau, instrumental in the continuation of the covenant line through Jacob.

- **Leah** (Genesis 29–30; 49:31)
  Jacob's first wife, mother of six tribes of Israel, including Judah, from whom the Messiah would come.

- **Rachel** (Genesis 29–35)
  Jacob's beloved wife and mother of Joseph and Benjamin, whose life reflects longing, rivalry, and God's sovereignty.

---

### **Exodus**

- **Miriam** (Exodus 2:4–8; 15:20–21; Numbers 12)
  Sister of Moses and Aaron, a prophetess who helped lead Israel and praised God after the Red Sea crossing.

---

### **Judges**

- **Deborah** (Judges 4–5)
  A prophetess and judge of Israel who led with wisdom and faith, delivering Israel from Canaanite oppression.

---

### **Ruth**

- **Naomi** (Ruth 1–4)
  A widow whose journey from bitterness to restoration reflects God's providence and faithfulness.

- **Ruth** (Ruth 1–4)
  A Moabite woman whose loyalty, faith, and obedience brought her into the lineage of King David and Jesus Christ.

---

### **1 Samuel**

- **Hannah** (1 Samuel 1–2)
  A woman of deep prayer and faith whose vow to God led to the birth of Samuel, a great prophet and judge.

- **Abigail** (1 Samuel 25)
  A wise and discerning woman whose actions prevented bloodshed and later became the wife of David.

---

### **2 Samuel**

- **Bathsheba** (2 Samuel 11–12; 1 Kings 1–2)
  Wife of David and mother of Solomon, whose story includes sin, repentance, and God's redemptive purposes.

---

### **Esther**

- **Esther** (Esther 1–10)
  A Jewish queen who courageously risked her life to save her people, revealing God's providence behind the scenes.

---

### **Job**

- **Job's Wife** (Job 2:9–10)
  An unnamed figure whose response to suffering contrasts with Job's perseverance and faith in God.

---

## New Testament

### **Matthew**

- **Mary (Mother of Jesus)** (Matthew 1–2; 13:55)
  Chosen by God to bear the Messiah, she exemplifies humility, obedience, and faith.

- **Mary Magdalene** (Matthew 27:56–61; 28:1–10)
  A devoted follower of Jesus and one of the first witnesses of His resurrection.

---

### **Mark**

- **Salome** (Mark 15:40; 16:1)
  A follower of Jesus who was present at the crucifixion and among those who came to anoint His body.

- **Mary Magdalene** (Mark 15:40–47; 16:1–9)
  A faithful disciple delivered by Christ and a witness to His death and resurrection.

---

### **Luke**

- **Elizabeth** (Luke 1)
  Wife of Zechariah and mother of John the Baptist, noted for her righteousness and faith.

- **Mary (Mother of Jesus)** (Luke 1–2; 8:19–21)
  A humble servant of God who treasured His promises and trusted His plan.

- **Joanna** (Luke 8:1–3; 24:10)
  A supporter of Jesus' ministry and a witness to the empty tomb.

- **Martha** (Luke 10:38–42)
  A devoted follower of Jesus whose story emphasizes spiritual priorities over anxious busyness.

- **Mary of Bethany** (Luke 10:38–42; cf. John 11–12)
  A woman devoted to Jesus, known for sitting at His feet and anointing Him in worship.

- **Mary Magdalene** (Luke 8:2–3; 24:10)
  A transformed follower of Christ and witness of His resurrection.

---

### **John**

- **Martha** (John 11–12)
  A woman of growing faith who confessed Jesus as "the Christ, the Son of God."

- **Mary of Bethany** (John 11–12)
  Known for her deep devotion and worship, honoring Jesus before His death.

- **Mary Magdalene** (John 19:25; 20:1–18)
  The first to see the risen Christ and commissioned to tell the disciples.

---

### **Acts**

- **Priscilla** (Acts 18)
  A knowledgeable teacher who, with her husband Aquila, helped instruct early believers.

- **Lydia** (Acts 16:14–15, 40)
  The first recorded convert in Europe and a supporter of the early church.

---

### **Romans**

- **Phoebe** (Romans 16:1–2)
  A servant of the church at Cenchrea and a trusted helper of the apostle Paul.

---

### **Summary**
- Approximate number of major women listed: **25**
- These women played vital roles in God's redemptive plan, demonstrating faith, obedience, courage, wisdom, and devotion throughout Scripture.
